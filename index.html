Frequently Asked Questions

Who is eligible to play?

There is no minimum age for our tournaments. All players must have an active DUPR account to participate. Age-based divisions use your current calendar year age:

  • 50+ Division: Players who turn 50 or older at any point in the current year.
  • 65+ Division: Players who turn 65 or older at any point in the current year.
How are skill levels and brackets organized?

Skill brackets use team average DUPR caps at the upper end of each bracket. For example, in a 3.0–3.5 bracket, your team's average DUPR must be 3.5 or below.

Fair play is non-negotiable. Players who intentionally misrepresent their skill level will be removed from the tournament and may be barred from future events. We use DUPR ratings to keep competition honest and give every team a fair chance to win.

How many matches is each team guaranteed?

Every entrant is scheduled to play at least 5 games total. Typical structure: 4 round-robin games + at least 1 playoff game.

  • Round-robin (pools of 5+ teams): Games to 11, win by 1.
  • Round-robin (pools of 4 or fewer): Games to 15, win by 1.
  • Playoff games: To 15, win by 2.
  • Medal matches (1st/2nd and 3rd/4th): Single game to 15, win by 2.
What does it cost and how do we register?

Players must register as a team — solo registrations are not accepted. Each player pays individually; pricing is shown on each event’s registration page. Register through the CourtReserve links on the schedule.

How do I find a partner?

Ask in your local pickleball community, post on Montgomery County pickleball groups, or email the tournament director if you need help connecting with a player at your level.

What is the refund or cancellation policy?

Refund and credit policies are set by the host facility. Check your registration confirmation for the contact email at your venue, or email tournaments@sammorrispb.com for help.

Can I play up a division?

Yes. You can enter a higher bracket as long as your team average DUPR stays below the cap for that bracket. For example, in a 3.0–3.5 bracket, your team average DUPR must be 3.5 or below.

What if my partner drops out?

You’re responsible for finding a replacement whose DUPR fits the bracket cap. If you can’t find a replacement, email tournaments@sammorrispb.com at least 72 hours before the event to request a credit toward a future tournament.

What if we're late or don't show up?

Teams that are 15 minutes late forfeit the first game. Each additional 15 minutes forfeits the next game. A full no-show is treated as forfeited games under the same rule.

What's the tournament format?

Round robin pools followed by a playoff bracket. Format scales based on registration. The minimum number of teams needed to run a bracket is 4 teams.

  • 4 teams: 3 round-robin games (one against each other team), games to 11 straight (win by 1). Teams are then seeded into a double-elimination bracket, games to 15 win by 2.

Every team gets competitive play regardless of bracket size.

What are the rules?

All tournaments follow USA official rules. Matches are self-officiated — players make their own calls with honesty and respect. When in doubt on line calls, give the benefit to your opponent.

What should I bring?

Paddle (plus a backup), comfortable court shoes, water bottle, snacks, and a positive attitude. Check your registration confirmation for any venue-specific concessions.

Where do I park?

Parking varies by venue. Check your registration confirmation email for the specific instructions for your event.

What if a tournament is full?

If a bracket is sold out, check back — spots open when teams withdraw. You can also email Tournament Director Sam Morris at tournaments@sammorrispb.com to be placed on a waitlist. Waitlisted teams are contacted in order as spots become available.

What happens if there’s severe weather or an emergency?

All Link & Dink tournaments are held on indoor courts, so weather rarely affects play. In the unlikely event of a facility closure or emergency cancellation, all registered teams will receive a full credit for a future event. We’ll notify all participants via email as soon as possible.

Will I get a reminder before the tournament?

Yes! You’ll receive a confirmation email the day before your tournament with check-in time, court assignments, and any last-minute updates. Make sure the email on your CourtReserve account is up to date.

What makes Link & Dink tournaments different?

We're community-first. Every opponent is a potential future partner. We emphasize connection over pure competition, growth over glory, and positive sportsmanship. After each match, we encourage exchanging contact info to keep playing together.

Who do I contact with questions?

Before the event: For questions, changes, or concerns before the start of the event, email Tournament Director Sam Morris at tournaments@sammorrispb.com.

Day-of questions: On tournament day, questions about schedule, courts, disputes, or logistics should be directed to the staff member on site.

Where are the tournaments held?

Tournament location is listed on each event’s registration page.

What are the court, food, and parking details?

All tournaments are played on indoor, dedicated courts. Check your registration confirmation for venue-specific food, beverage, and parking details.

How can I support our sponsors?

Our tournaments are made possible in part by our community sponsors — JOOLA! Please take a moment during the event to visit sponsor tables, learn about their services, and thank them for supporting local play. If you’re interested in sponsoring a future tournament, contact the tournament director or facility for current opportunities.
