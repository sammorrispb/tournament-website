What Makes Link & Dink Tournaments Different

At Link & Dink, tournaments aren’t just about winning — they’re about growing together. Every match is an opportunity to improve your game, meet compatible partners, and strengthen our community.

The ladder is the prize, but the partners you meet are the point. Every round-robin round puts you in front of 3–4 new players at your level. By the end of the season, you’ll have competed with 50+ players in Montgomery County — and half of them will become regular playing partners. That’s the real championship: a bench of people who’ve earned your trust across 20 tournaments on premium Dill Dinkers courts.

Our Philosophy

  • Connection-focused competition where every opponent is a potential future partner
  • Growth over glory with emphasis on learning and skill development
  • Community building through positive sportsmanship and mutual support
  • Discovery pathway from meeting players to forming lasting partnerships

Tournament Formats

Round Robin Play + Brackets

Format depends on the number of teams registered. The typical structure:

  • 4 initial round robin games
  • All teams advance to single elimination bracket

Scoring & Match Rules

Standard Scoring

  • Round robin matches: Games to 11, win by 1
  • Bracket matches: Games to 15, win by 2
  • Switch sides: At 6 points in games to 11, at 8 points in games to 15
  • Timeouts: One 60-second timeout per team per game

Match Etiquette

  • 5-minute warm-up before your first match
  • 2-minute break between games
  • Generous line calls — when in doubt, favor your opponent
  • Self-officiated — players make their own calls with honesty and respect

Official Rules: Link & Dink tournaments follow USA Pickleball (USAPA) Official Rules for all gameplay. Refer to the current USAPA rulebook for complete regulations.

Line Call Guidelines

  • Call your own side only — never call lines on opponent’s side
  • Make calls immediately and clearly — don’t wait or hesitate
  • When uncertain: Give benefit of doubt to your opponent
  • “Out” calls must be made before the ball bounces again or is hit

Self-Officiating Standards

  • Call your own faults immediately — foot faults, net touches, etc.
  • Announce faults clearly so all players hear
  • Don’t wait for opponents to notice your violations
  • Ask for clarification if unsure about a rule

Dispute Resolution

  • Stay calm and respectful during disagreements
  • Refer to USAPA rules when possible
  • Call tournament director if agreement cannot be reached
  • Accept referee decisions as final
  • Replay the point if fault timing is unclear

The Link & Dink Way

Before Each Match

  1. Introduce yourself to opponents and partner
  2. Share your skill level honestly
  3. Set a positive tone: “Let’s have a great match!”
  4. Rock, paper, scissors to determine serve/side

During Play

  • Compete with respect — play your best while maintaining sportsmanship
  • Celebrate good shots from all players
  • Communicate clearly with your partner
  • Stay positive through wins and losses

After Each Match

  1. Thank opponents for a good game
  2. Exchange contact info with compatible players
  3. Share constructive feedback if asked
  4. Connect on the Link & Dink platform to stay in touch

Community Standards

Sportsmanship Expectations

  • Honest line calls even when it costs you points
  • Positive attitude regardless of score
  • Encouragement for partners and opponents
  • Inclusive behavior welcoming to all skill levels

Growth Mindset

  • Ask questions and seek tips from stronger players
  • Offer help to players developing their skills
  • Learn from losses — every match teaches something
  • Celebrate improvement over perfection

Partnership Building

  • Be open to playing with different partners
  • Exchange information with compatible players
  • Follow up after tournaments to maintain connections

Divisions & Skill Levels

Self-Rating Guidelines

Be honest about your current skill level. Consider recent play rather than your best day ever. Ask for community validation if uncertain. Accurate ratings create better, more enjoyable matches.

Division Structure

Intermediate (3.0–3.5)

Solid basics, working on strategy and placement

Intermediate Advanced (3.5–4.0)

Developing consistent variety of skills and strategies with some tournament experience

Advanced (4.0+)

Strong skills, competitive strategy, plenty of tournament experience

Tournament Day Logistics

What to Bring

  • Primary paddle and backup
  • Comfortable court shoes with good grip
  • Water and snacks for energy
  • Positive attitude and openness to new connections
  • Contact info ready to share with new partners

Check-In Process

  • Arrive 15–30 minutes early for check-in and warm-up
  • Confirm your division and partnership
  • Review tournament bracket and schedule
  • Get court assignments and emergency procedures

Schedule Flexibility

  • Matches run on time — be ready when called
  • Brief delays possible to accommodate close matches
  • Stay engaged between your matches to support community
  • Awards ceremony follows final matches

Connection Opportunities

Mentor System

  • Play up: Offer to pay entry fees to partner with higher-level players
  • Mentor others: Offer your skills to help developing players
  • Post requests on Link & Dink: Looking for specific types of partnerships or coaching — www.linkanddink.com

Partnership Formation

  • Wolf Pack development: Find your regular playing group
  • Skill-based matching: Connect with players at complementary levels
  • Style compatibility: Find players who match your competitive approach
  • Future play planning: Schedule ongoing games with new connections

Tournament Success Tips

Competitive Excellence

  • Warm up properly before your first match
  • Stay hydrated and fuel your body appropriately
  • Focus on fundamentals under pressure
  • Communicate effectively with your partner
  • Adapt strategy based on opponents’ strengths

Community Building

  • Learn everyone’s name you play with or against
  • Ask about others’ journey during breaks
  • Share your experience and what brought you to the sport
  • Plan follow-up play with compatible partners
  • Join the Link & Dink platform to maintain connections

After the Tournament

Immediate Actions

  • Connect with new partners while contact info is fresh
  • Schedule follow-up play within a week if possible
  • Update your Link & Dink profile with new connections
  • Reflect on lessons learned and areas for improvement

Continuing Your Journey

  • Practice skills learned from stronger players
  • Maintain new relationships through regular play
  • Consider coaching if you discovered significant skill gaps
  • Sign up for future tournaments to continue growing

Questions or Issues

During the Tournament

  • Court monitors available for rules clarifications
  • Tournament director handles disputes and scheduling
  • First aid station for any injuries or medical needs

Post-Tournament

  • Link & Dink platform for connection follow-up
  • Tournament organizer for feedback or future event questions
  • Community support through Link & Dink app and groups

Every Link & Dink tournament is an opportunity to compete at your best while building lasting relationships. When you leave feeling more connected and excited, we’ve all succeeded together.
